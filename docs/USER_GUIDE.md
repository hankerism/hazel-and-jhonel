# Your Wedding Website — A Guide for Hazel & Jhonel 💛

Welcome! This guide explains everything you can do with your wedding website
and dashboard, in plain language. No technical knowledge needed.

**Your website** is the beautiful page your guests see — your story, the
schedule, photos, and the RSVP form.

**Your dashboard** is your private control room. Everything you change there
appears on the live website the moment you save.

---

## 1. Logging In

1. Go to your website address and add `/login` at the end
   (for example: `yourwebsite.com/login`).
2. Sign in one of two ways:
   - **Password** — your email and password.
   - **Email Link** — switch to the "Email Link" tab, enter your email, and
     we'll send you a sign-in link. Click it within an hour and you're in.
     No password needed.
3. Forgot your password? Click **Forgot password?** on the login page,
   enter your email, and follow the link in the message to choose a new one.

When you're done, use **Sign out** at the top right. If you leave the
dashboard open for a long time, it may sign you out for safety — just sign
in again.

> Only the two of you have accounts. Guests can never see the dashboard —
> they can only see the website and submit their RSVP.

## 2. Editing Website Content

Open the dashboard and use the menu on the left. Every page works the same
way: make your change, press **Save**, and it's live on the website
immediately. If something goes wrong, a message appears explaining what to
fix.

**Wedding Details** — your names, the date and times, venues and addresses,
dress code, wedding colors, the parking note, your welcome message, and the
big photo at the top of the website (the "hero image"). This is also where
the countdown gets its date from.

**Story** — the "Moments That Led Here" section. Add, edit, delete, or drag
to reorder your milestones. Each one has a title, a short story, and an
optional photo.

## 3. Managing FAQs

**FAQs** in the menu. These are the questions guests see near the bottom of
the site ("What is the dress code?", "Can I bring a plus one?"…).

- **Add** — write the question and its answer, save.
- **Edit / Delete** — use the buttons on each entry.
- **Reorder** — drag entries into the order you like.

## 4. Managing the Gallery

**Gallery** in the menu. Each photo needs a **link to an image** (a web
address ending in the picture itself) and an optional caption that appears
when guests view it. You can delete photos and drag them into your
preferred order. The photos appear on the website in exactly the order you
see them here.

## 5. Managing the Schedule

**Schedule** in the menu — the "Order of the Day" on your website
(Guest Arrival 3:30 PM, Ceremony 4:00 PM, and so on). Each item has a time,
a title, and an optional description. Add, edit, delete, and drag to
reorder, just like FAQs.

## 6. Managing the RSVP Form

**RSVP Form** in the menu. This controls the actual form guests fill in —
you can shape it without touching anything technical:

- **Fields** — for each question on the form (phone number, meal
  preference, song request…), choose whether it's shown, whether it's
  required, and what the label says. *First name, last name, and email are
  always on* — the system needs them to keep responses organized and to
  stop duplicates.
- **Meal options** — the menu guests choose from (currently Beef, Chicken,
  Fish, Vegetarian). Add, rename, remove, or reorder anytime.
- **Settings** — the maximum party size (up to 20), the RSVP deadline shown
  on the site, whether guests may decline through the form, and whether the
  "plus one" question only appears for parties of two or more.

## 7. Reviewing RSVPs

The moment a guest submits an RSVP, **you get an email** —
"🎉 New RSVP Received" — with everything they said and a **Review RSVP**
button that takes you straight to the dashboard.

**RSVP Responses** in the menu shows every response:

- **Search** by name or email (tip: press the `/` key to jump to the search
  box).
- **Filter** by accepted/declined, or by status.
- **Click any row** to open the full response: their contact details, party
  size, meal choice, dietary notes, song request, and any message they
  wrote you.
- **Export CSV** downloads a spreadsheet of whatever the list is currently
  showing — handy for the caterer or seating chart.

The **Overview** page (the first page of the dashboard) totals everything
up: how many accepted, how many declined, and how many seats to set.

Every new response starts as **Pending** — meaning "waiting for you to look
at it."

## 8. Confirming Guests

When you've reviewed a response, open it and choose a status:

- **Confirmed** — you're happy with it and the guest is officially on the
  list. **If they accepted, this automatically sends them a beautiful
  confirmation email** with all the wedding details and buttons to add the
  day to their calendar. This happens only the first time — confirming
  again later won't re-email them.
- **Contacted** — useful when you've spoken with the guest directly and
  just want to mark that.
- **Pending** — back to "not reviewed yet."

Declined guests can be confirmed too (to mark them reviewed) — they simply
don't get a confirmation email.

## 9. Resending Emails

Open any **confirmed, accepted** response and look at the **Confirmation
Email** section at the bottom of the panel. You'll see whether the email
was **Sent** (with the date), **Failed** (with the reason under "Last
error"), or **Not Sent** yet.

Press **Resend Confirmation Email** whenever you need to — for example if
the guest says they never got it, or if the first attempt failed. It sends
a fresh copy of the same email.

## 10. Sending a Test Email

Want to see exactly what guests receive? Go to **Settings** → the **Email**
card → **Send Test Email**. The real confirmation email (with sample guest
details) is sent to *your own* inbox. If it arrives, everything is working.

The card also shows which address your emails are sent from, and a green
dot when email is set up correctly.

## 11. Common Troubleshooting

**"I made a change but the website still shows the old version."**
Changes go live when you save. Refresh the page on the website; on your
phone, close and reopen the browser tab. If you never pressed Save (check
for the "Saved" indicator at the top of the dashboard), the change wasn't
stored.

**"A guest says they can't RSVP — it says they're already on the list."**
Each email address can only RSVP once — that's how we prevent duplicates.
If their details genuinely changed (party size, meal), there's no guest
self-edit: note the change and adjust plans directly, or have them reach
out to you (the form tells them to contact you).

**"The confirmation email failed."**
Open the response and read **Last error** — it says what happened in plain
words (for example, the email service couldn't be reached). The guest's
RSVP is safe either way. Try **Resend** after a few minutes. If every email
fails, use **Send Test Email** in Settings; if that fails too, the email
account setup needs attention — contact whoever set up the website and read
them the error message.

**"I didn't get the 'New RSVP' notification email."**
Check spam/promotions first. The notification goes to
**hazelandjhonel@gmail.com**. The RSVP itself is never lost — it will
always be in RSVP Responses even if the notification email had a problem.

**"I got signed out."**
Normal after long idle periods. Sign back in — nothing is lost.

**"The sign-in link in my email doesn't work."**
Links expire after an hour and work only once. Request a fresh one from the
login page.

**"The music doesn't autoplay for me."**
Some phones and browsers block autoplay until the visitor taps the page —
that's a browser rule, not a problem with your site. The floating music
button always lets guests play or pause.

**Something else?** Contact whoever maintains the website — and mention any
message you saw on screen, word for word. Every error in the system is
written to be readable and specific.
